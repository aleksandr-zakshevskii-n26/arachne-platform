## Opportunity: Pattern Sequence Predictor

**Target user:** Children aged 7–13 and their parents, spanning two contexts: (1) homework help when a child is stuck on "what comes next?" problems, and (2) test preparation for exams that heavily feature number patterns (UK 11+, Math Olympiad, NAPLAN, US state standardized tests).

**Problem:** Number pattern questions appear in every math curriculum and in high-stakes tests, yet no child-friendly tool exists to explain *how* a pattern works. OEIS is for mathematicians. Wolfram Alpha outputs formula notation. Worksheet sites provide practice but never explain the answer. When a child is stuck on "2, 6, 18, 54, ?" they have nowhere to go except ask an adult — and many adults can't articulate the rule clearly either.

**Our angle:** A single-purpose tool where a child types in 4–6 numbers and instantly sees: (1) the predicted next number, (2) a plain-English explanation ("Each number is multiplied by 3"), and (3) a visual number line with annotated operation arrows showing the rule in action. No formulas, no variables — just words and pictures. The MVP detects 8–10 common pattern types: constant addition, constant subtraction, constant multiplication, constant division, squares, triangular numbers, Fibonacci-like (each term = sum of previous two), alternating operations, and simple powers. For unrecognized patterns, it says honestly: "I don't recognize this pattern yet — try checking with your teacher."

**MVP scope:** Entirely client-side (HTML/CSS/JS, no backend). One input field for a comma-separated sequence. One output screen with the answer, explanation, and animated SVG number line. Free tier handles unlimited lookups. Paid tier ($2.99/month) adds: timed practice mode (generates patterns at adjustable difficulty), shareable challenge links (create a pattern puzzle, send to a friend), and a progress tracker. Build time: 4–6 weeks for a solo developer.

**Why now:** The gap in the market is a genuine vacancy, not a narrow wedge. Test-prep spending is resilient in any economy because the stakes (school admissions, competition placement) don't diminish. The sharing mechanic creates organic virality with zero marketing spend. And the tool is a perfect "first product" for Arachne — it demonstrates smart, delightful, single-concept design and establishes a brand identity that's distinct from generic math platforms.

**Risks:** (1) Rule detection has a long tail — unusual patterns will produce "I don't know" responses, which could frustrate users. Mitigated by constraining scope and being transparent. (2) A competitor could replicate the approach quickly since the math is not proprietary. Mitigated by moving fast and building the community/sharing features as a moat. (3) Seasonality — test-prep demand spikes around exam periods. Mitigated by the homework-help use case providing baseline traffic year-round.

**Recommended next step:** Product director to make a go/no-go decision on this brief. If go: define the exact list of 8–10 pattern types for MVP, wireframe the single-screen UI, and assign to a front-end developer for a 5-week sprint.
