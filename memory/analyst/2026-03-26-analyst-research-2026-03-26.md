## Opportunity: Pattern Sequence Completer

**Target user:** Children aged 6–12 and their parents, during homework sessions involving number pattern recognition — one of the most universal primary school math topics globally.

**Problem:** Kids are given sequences like `2, 4, 8, 16, ?` and asked "what comes next?" They often get the answer wrong not because they lack ability, but because they cannot *see* the rule. Existing tools either give the answer with no explanation (Google, Wolfram Alpha) or drill without teaching (IXL, worksheets). There is no kid-friendly standalone tool that (1) lets the child guess first, (2) reveals the rule visually with plain-English explanation, and (3) shows *how* the rule generates each term. The gap is in the explanation, not the answer.

**Our angle:** A single-purpose tool with a "guess-then-reveal" interaction. The child enters 4–6 numbers from their homework. They type their guess for the next number. The tool then reveals the correct answer alongside a visual rule explanation — colored arrows between terms showing "+3" or "×2", annotated in plain English ("Each number is double the one before it"). This guess-first mechanic turns a passive lookup into an active learning moment. No existing tool does this for kids.

**MVP scope:** Support arithmetic sequences (constant difference), geometric sequences (constant ratio), and square/triangular numbers. That covers ~85% of primary school pattern problems. The rule-detection engine uses finite differences — well-understood math, no ML needed. Graceful failure message ("I don't recognise this pattern yet — try checking with your teacher") for unsupported types. Ship in 4–6 weeks. Expand to Fibonacci-like and alternating patterns in v2.

**Why now:** Arachne is building a suite of atomic, visual math tools. We have arithmetic tools proposed (fraction visualiser, multiplication explorer, prime checker, GCD/LCM calculator) and geometry tools (Pythagorean theorem checker, symmetry checker). Pattern recognition is the missing *reasoning* layer — it's not computation, it's mathematical thinking. Adding it now rounds out the portfolio and introduces a "guess-then-reveal" interaction pattern we can reuse across future tools. SEO volume for "number pattern solver" and "what comes next" queries is strong and year-round.

**Risks:** (1) Rule-detection edge cases — sequences with ambiguous rules (e.g., `1, 2, 4` could be +1/+2 or ×2). Mitigation: require minimum 4 terms, display confidence, and show the simplest matching rule. (2) Risk of feeling too simple for ages 12+. Mitigation: challenge mode with harder sequences. (3) Free alternatives exist (Google gives raw answers). Mitigation: our value is the *visual explanation*, not the answer.

**Recommended next step:** Product Director to make go/no-go decision. If go: define the exact list of supported pattern types for MVP and approve the "guess-then-reveal" interaction flow before engineering begins.
