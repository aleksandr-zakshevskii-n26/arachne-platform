## Opportunity: Multiplication Area Model Builder

**Target user:** Children aged 8–11 learning multi-digit multiplication, and their parents who need to help with homework but don't recognise the "box method" taught in modern curricula (Common Core, UK National Curriculum, Singapore Math).

**Problem:** Multi-digit multiplication is now widely taught using the area model (a.k.a. box method), where numbers are decomposed by place value and multiplied as rectangular areas. Parents who learned the traditional algorithm are confused and cannot help. Children who miss the concept in class have nowhere to go for a clear, interactive visual explanation. The best existing tool (Brainingcamp) is locked behind school subscriptions. Free alternatives are static images or YouTube videos — nothing lets a child type in their actual homework problem and see it decomposed step by step.

**Our angle:** A free, instant, zero-login tool where a child types two numbers (e.g. 23 × 14) and sees an animated area model: the rectangle splits into sub-rectangles by place value (20×10, 20×4, 3×10, 3×4), each labelled and colour-coded, with partial products summing to the final answer. A plain-English sidebar explains each step. A parent reading along understands the method in 30 seconds. The child points at the screen and says "oh, that's why."

**MVP scope:** Single page. Two number inputs (up to 3 digits each). Animated SVG/canvas rectangle decomposition. Step-by-step text explanation panel. Print-friendly view for worksheets. No backend required for core functionality. A focused developer ships a polished MVP in 4–6 weeks. Revenue layer: optional $2/month for printable practice worksheets and a "check my work" mode where the child fills in the boxes themselves.

**Why now:** Area model multiplication is the single most-Googled "new math" method by confused parents. Search intent is high, recurring (weekly homework for 2+ years per child), and peaks predictably during the school year. No free interactive tool serves this query well. First mover with strong SEO captures a durable organic channel. This tool also establishes the core Arachne pattern — input a problem, see a visual explanation — that generalises to long division, fraction addition, and dozens of other concepts.

**Risks:** (1) Teachers may prefer students struggle without a solver — mitigated by the "check my work" mode that requires the child to fill in boxes first. (2) The topic, while large, is still one concept — mitigated by treating this as the first in a series. (3) Seasonal search volume dips in summer — mitigated by targeting Southern Hemisphere markets during Northern summer.

**Recommended next step:** Product director to decide go/no-go on building an MVP. If go: define exact input constraints (max digits), confirm animation style (step-by-step vs. all-at-once), and decide whether the free tier includes printing.
