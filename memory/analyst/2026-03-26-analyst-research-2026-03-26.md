## Opportunity: Grid Symmetry Painter

**Target user:** Children aged 7–11 (and their parents/teachers) learning about reflective symmetry in primary school maths.

**Problem:** Symmetry is one of the most visual topics in the curriculum, yet kids are taught it with static worksheets — draw the other half of a butterfly, complete the pattern across a dotted line. The concept clicks when children *see* reflection happen in real time, but no free, polished browser tool lets them paint on a grid and watch the mirror image appear instantly. Existing options (Transum, Busythings) are either dated, rigid, or locked behind paywalls and app stores.

**Our angle:** A single-page web tool with a colourable grid and a draggable axis of symmetry. The child paints cells on one side; the mirrored cells light up instantly on the other. Two modes: (1) **Create mode** — free painting to make symmetrical pixel art, exportable as an image. (2) **Challenge mode** — a half-pattern is shown and the child must complete the reflection, with immediate right/wrong feedback. The "aha" moment is visceral: you tap a cell, its twin appears across the line. Symmetry stops being a rule and becomes something you *feel*.

**MVP scope:** One developer, 2–3 weeks. Core: HTML/CSS grid (8×8 default, resizable to 12×12), colour palette (6 colours), vertical or horizontal mirror axis, paint-on-click with instant reflection. Challenge mode: 10 pre-built half-patterns with difficulty levels. Export: download grid as PNG. No backend required — static site, all logic client-side. Optional fast-follow: diagonal symmetry axis, teacher dashboard to create custom challenges.

**Why now:** (1) The competitive gap is real and unusually clean — there is no free, browser-based, real-time symmetry painting tool aimed at children. (2) The pixel-art aesthetic is culturally resonant with kids right now (Minecraft, pixel art TikTok). (3) The shareability of created artwork drives organic discovery without any marketing spend. (4) This is the ideal first tool to demonstrate Arachne's thesis: one concept, one tool, instant delight.

**Risks:** (1) Symmetry is a narrow curriculum topic — usage may spike during that unit and drop. Mitigation: creative mode extends engagement beyond homework. (2) Low inherent stickiness as a free tool — monetisation depends on building teacher features (custom challenges, class sharing) that aren't in MVP. (3) A competitor could clone this quickly — speed to market and polish are the only moat.

**Recommended next step:** Product-director to decide: (a) approve for build sprint, or (b) request a lightweight prototype (one-day spike) to validate the interaction feel before committing to full MVP.
